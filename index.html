3-Day Donegal Family Tour

Base Location: 1 Loughcrillan Rd, Glenties, Co. Donegal, F94 KT22

Day 1: Southwest Donegal

Slieve League Cliffs

Distance: ~50 km | Drive Time: ~1 hour
Things to do: Enjoy panoramic views from Bunglass Viewpoint, walk along the Pilgrim's Path, visit the viewing platform. Expect dramatic cliff edges and sea breezes — bring warm clothing!

Breathtaking high sea cliffs with panoramic Atlantic views.

Killybegs Harbour

Distance: ~30 km | Drive Time: ~35 minutes
Things to do: Take a stroll along the fishing port, watch the boats, dine in a local seafood restaurant. Expect fresh catches, scenic waters, and a bustling harbor vibe.

Scenic fishing port with fresh seafood and a lively quay.

Route on Google Maps

Glencolmcille Folk Village

Distance: ~60 km | Drive Time: ~1 hr 15 min
Things to do: Tour replica cottages from the 18th–20th centuries, view traditional tools and furnishings, visit the craft shop. Expect a step back in time with a warm welcome.

Open-air museum of traditional Irish cottages through history.

Day 2: Nature & Relaxation

Glenveagh National Park

Distance: ~65 km | Drive Time: ~1 hr 10 min
Things to do: Visit Glenveagh Castle and gardens, walk or cycle along the trails, spot red deer and birds. Expect peaceful nature and family-friendly routes.

Castle, gardens, and tranquil lakes in a vast nature reserve.

Mount Errigal Viewpoint

Distance: ~50 km | Drive Time: ~1 hour
Things to do: Take short hikes or photos at the base, admire the reflection on nearby lakes. Expect windy conditions and amazing sunset shots.

Donegal’s tallest peak, popular for views and gentle trails.

Letterkenny (Dinner)

Distance: ~70 km | Drive Time: ~1 hr 20 min
Things to do: Choose from local restaurants, visit An Grianán Theatre or a local pub. Expect a vibrant evening with food and light entertainment.

Vibrant town with restaurants and shops for a relaxed evening.

Grianán of Aileach

Distance: ~100 km | Drive Time: ~1 hr 40 min
Things to do: Explore the ancient stone ring fort, enjoy panoramic views, learn about its early medieval history. Expect peace, quiet, and stunning landscapes.

Ancient hilltop stone ring fort with stunning 360° views.

Distance: ~105 km | Drive Time: ~1 hr 50 min

General Tips for Visiting Donegal

  • Weather: Be prepared for rapid changes—bring waterproofs and layers.
  • Driving: Roads can be narrow and winding; take it slow and enjoy the views.
  • Cash: Some remote areas may not accept cards—carry a small amount of cash.
  • Fuel: Fill up in larger towns when possible; petrol stations can be sparse in rural areas.
  • Connectivity: Mobile signal may be weak in some remote parts—download maps ahead of time.
  • Respect nature: Leave no trace and watch out for sheep on roads!
